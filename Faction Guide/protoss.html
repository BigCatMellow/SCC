Protoss field guide

Protoss

Elite, technical, and position-sensitive. Protoss tends to win by making fewer but higher-value activations matter more. Protect key pieces, stage around support infrastructure, and leverage placement or energy tricks to get decisive value from elite units.
Why this race needs a guide layer
Protoss cards frequently hinge on placement wording, support pieces, or timing windows that look simple until they collide with core movement rules.
Read this race by layers

Start with the faction cards to understand the army frame. Then read the tactical cards as infrastructure. Only after that should you read the unit cards, because many unit evaluations change once the right support shell exists.

This guide's method

Every entry gets a plain-English read first. Selective RAW watch boxes appear only where wording is likely to matter at the table.

Faction Card

Daelaam

Faction Card
Faction CardFaction CardUnique

Resource +1 EN; Slots: Elite slot 1, Core slot 3, Support slot 1.

Plain-English reading

Daelaam sets the army frame first and the play pattern second. It gives +1 EN when exhausted and opens Elite 1, Core 3, Support 1. Its key faction tools are Dae’Uhl (Reaction); Mass Recall (Active).

Role in the army

Force-multiplier faction card with strong board-position leverage.

Ability summary
  • Any Phase — Dae’Uhl (Reaction): When a Friendly Unit receives Damage, reduce the Total Damage by 2 (to a minimum of 1).
  • Movement Phase — Mass Recall (Active): Once per Game. Place a Faction Indicator anywhere on the battlefield. All Friendly Protoss Units Within 6" of this Faction Indicator are removed from the battlefield and placed in Reserves .
Strengths
  • changes positioning or deployment in ways raw statlines cannot
Watch-outs
  • needs the board to compress before it gets full value
  • some of its swingiest value is limited-use
  • wants support spacing and can lose value if isolated
Synergy notes
  • Pairs best with lists that can spare energy repeatedly instead of spending every card on raw payment.
  • Gets stronger when the rest of the army can exploit sudden board repositioning instead of merely admiring it.
Matchup watch
  • Protoss: Expect high-value trades and support-piece wars. Deny clean warp, power, or recall setups before the elite mirror snowballs.
  • Terran: Respect ranged attrition and command-point tempo. Use terrain and timing to avoid getting stranded in open lanes under disciplined fire.
  • Zerg: Do not let the board sprawl for free. Your elite quality matters most when you can cut off creep, summons, and recursive entry points early.

How to study this race well
  • Read the faction cards first. They tell you what kind of game the list wants to play.
  • Read tactical cards second. They explain which slot pressure, resource pressure, and infrastructure tricks the race can actually support.
  • Only then read unit cards. A unit's real value often depends on whether the support shell described above is present.
  • Use RAW watch boxes only when the plain-English summary is not enough to settle a table question.
