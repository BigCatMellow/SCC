Terran field guide

Terran

Efficient, combined-arms, and infrastructure-driven. Terran usually plays the cleanest 'build support, then exploit it' game. Open lanes with tactical cards, stack command-point value, and let versatile ranged units trade up while support pieces hold the machine together.
Why this race needs a guide layer
Terran cards often look straightforward but hide real timing and positioning leverage in command-point spending, token pieces, and deployment tricks.
Read this race by layers

Start with the faction cards to understand the army frame. Then read the tactical cards as infrastructure. Only after that should you read the unit cards, because many unit evaluations change once the right support shell exists.

This guide's method

Every entry gets a plain-English read first. Selective RAW watch boxes appear only where wording is likely to matter at the table.

Faction Card

Raynor's Raiders

Faction Card
Faction CardFaction CardUnique

Resource +1 CP; Slots: Hero slot 1, Core slot 3, Support slot 1.

Plain-English reading

Raynor's Raiders sets the army frame first and the play pattern second. It gives +1 CP when exhausted and opens Hero 1, Core 3, Support 1. Its key faction tools are Rapid Reinforcements (Active); Ready for Pickup? (Active).

Role in the army

Force-multiplier faction card with strong board-position leverage.

Ability summary
  • Movement Phase — Rapid Reinforcements (Active): Place a Friendly Point Defence Drone Unit anywhere on the battlefield, more than 1" away from any Enemy model. Remove this Unit at the End of the Round.
  • Movement Phase — Ready for Pickup? (Active): Once per Game. The active Unit resolves the PLACE (12) effect. Models placed by this effect cannot be set up Within the Engagement Range of any Enemy Unit. This resolves instead of performing a standard action.
Strengths
  • changes positioning or deployment in ways raw statlines cannot
Watch-outs
  • needs the board to compress before it gets full value
  • some of its swingiest value is limited-use
Synergy notes
  • Gets better as the list produces more reliable command-point turns.
  • Likes being backed by infrastructure cards that turn board access into actual pressure.
Matchup watch
  • Protoss: Do not feed elite counterpunches. Make Protoss cross open space or spend energy to reach your important guns.
  • Terran: Infrastructure and activation efficiency decide the mirror. Win the support war and your line pieces usually become better too.
  • Zerg: Do not let Zerg turn movement tricks into a flood. Kill the enabling pieces and force the swarm to fight through your best fire lanes.

How to study this race well
  • Read the faction cards first. They tell you what kind of game the list wants to play.
  • Read tactical cards second. They explain which slot pressure, resource pressure, and infrastructure tricks the race can actually support.
  • Only then read unit cards. A unit's real value often depends on whether the support shell described above is present.
  • Use RAW watch boxes only when the plain-English summary is not enough to settle a table question.
